Fibre optic vs Ethernet: Both use physical cables, but fibre sends data as pulses of light through glass strands rather than electrical signals through copper wire. This means far less interference, much lower latency, and speeds that can reach 10 Gbps or more — while standard Ethernet copper tops out around 1 Gbps for home connections.
5G vs 4G: 5G uses much higher radio frequencies (millimetre waves) which carry far more data — up to 20× faster than 4G. But those high frequencies don't travel as far or through walls as well, so 5G needs more towers close together. In cities you'll likely have 5G; in rural areas 4G is still common.
